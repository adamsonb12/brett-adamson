Tall Admin Tool

There are 5 Teaching Assisted Language Learning (TALL) applications at the Missionary Training Center (MTC). They are web applications built completely by us in the Technology department, and are used to help LDS missionaries become proficient in the language they are learning. Languages range from Spanish to Mandarin, and more continue to be added to the database.

Tall Admin Tool (TAT) is the TALL application controlling all content in the other TALL web applications. I've owned TAT since it's beginning, planning, designing, and writing user stories as well as monitoring its progress of being built and pushed into production by our programming team. It is built in Angular, IOS, and Andriod.

TAT provides TALL administration to filter, edit, and protect the content in their respective applications. It allows language experts to review content, edit it, and delete it, or add new content. It is the backbone of all other TALL applications.

Training

Training is being built with Angular into a responsive web-application. I meet almost daily with key stakeholders, my designer, and progamming team leads to improve Training and fulfill the stakeholders' requirements.

Wowza and Plex

Missionaries learning in the Missionary Training Center (MTC) often practice teaching in rooms with a video camera to record them. Missionaries and teachers use the recordings to improve their teaching skill.

Wowza is a video recording engine, and Plex is a media server, which let's users organize and view video content. I am testing the functionality of these applications with potential users. If successful, we will install the products in smaller MTC's all over the world, such as in Mexico, Brazil, and the Phillipines.

We've set up static IP cameras to connect with the Wowza server, which relays information and video streams to be stored and displayed in Plex.
